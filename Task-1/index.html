  • A Indian scientist and the 11th President of India, Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is also known by his initials A. P. J. Abdul Kalam. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15, 1931, and he died on July 27, 2015.

  • Dr. Kalam was a brilliant scientist and a visionary leader who significantly influenced India's space and missile programmes. He was also referred to as the "Missile Man of India" due to his crucial contribution to the advancement of ballistic missile technology in India.

  • He was not only a scientist but also a great teacher who inspired millions of young minds to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

  • Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's dedication to science and technology, his humility, and his commitment to the youth of India continue to inspire generations. He believed in the power of education and urged young minds to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.
